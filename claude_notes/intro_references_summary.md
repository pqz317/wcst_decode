## Notes — how the references group (consistent with the prose)

- **Frontoparietal "decision variable / template / belief" cluster** (Niv, Leong, Jahn, Xue, Kim, Enel, Bari, Tafazoli) spans human fMRI, macaque PFC/parietal, and mouse mPFC — ranging from single-variable perceptual decisions (Kim) to multi-task compositional inference (Tafazoli).
- **HPC/OFC "latent state, context, value map" cluster** (Knudsen, Elston, Zhou, Bakkour, Rueckemann) is the most explicitly cognitive-map / schema-framed.
- **ACC/striatum "rule & reward context" cluster** (Ebitz, Sarafyazd, Cohen, Chien, Hwang) centers on rule/context inference and representational geometry.
- **Abstract/distributed references** (Bernardi, Courellis; Findling, Steinmetz, Chen, Rosen) carry the geometry-of-abstraction and brain-wide-distribution threads, with Rosen as the framing review.

## Caveat

Several of these (Kim, Steinmetz, Chen, Bari) are **not** latent-variable *inference* tasks in the
strict sense — they are perceptual/value decisions or action coding rather than inference of hidden
states. If the point is specifically about *internal-model inference being distributed*, it may be
worth acknowledging that the brain-wide citations (Steinmetz, Chen) are about choice/action rather
than inferred latent states.
